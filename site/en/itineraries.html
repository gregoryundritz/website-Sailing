The South Shore & Estavayer

A peaceful cruise along the Grande Cariçaie, Switzerland’s largest lakeside nature reserve. Ideal for a full-day trip, with the possibility to anchor and swim on the beautiful sandbanks of the reserve.

  • Duration: 1 day
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Highlights: Nature reserve, sandbanks, Estavayer harbour

Grand Tour of the Lake

Set off for a 2- to 3-day cruise around the lake. Cross over to Gorgier, head up to Neuchâtel, then follow the north shore before heading back down to Cheyres.

  • Duration: 2 to 3 days (weekend)
  • Difficulty: Moderate
  • Places to see: Neuchâtel, Yverdon, views of the Alps

Heading South-West

Sailing towards the south-western end of the lake. Ideal for a quiet weekend with an overnight stay at the port of Grandson. A great opportunity to eat at a restaurant before heading back.

  • Duration: 1 to 2 days
  • Difficulty: Easy to Moderate
  • Highlights: Grandson Castle

Getaway to Portalban & Neuchâtel

A beautiful sail up the lake with the prevailing wind (South-West). Possible stop at the sandy port of Portalban, followed by a crossing to Neuchâtel and its historic area.

  • Duration: 1 to 2 days
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Highlights: Portalban beach, Neuchâtel harbour

Weather & Navigation

Wind Patterns on the Lake

Understand the main winds of Lake Neuchâtel to prepare your trips safely.

La Bise (North-East)

Fair weather wind, cold and dry. On Lake Neuchâtel, it quickly raises a short and steep chop. Ideal for sporty sailing, but requires vigilance as it can blow hard and create waves over 1.5 meters.

Le Joran (North-West)

Thermal wind descending from the Jura ridges. It typically rises in the late afternoon on hot summer days. Beware, it can be sudden, violent, and often heralds thunderstorms. It is advisable to seek shelter or reduce sail when it approaches.

Le Sud-Ouest (Vent Blanc)

The dominant and often most regular wind. It generally brings depressions or a clear sky (vent blanc). It is the perfect wind for long, peaceful crossings along the Grande Cariçaie towards Neuchâtel.
