General Terms and Conditions of Rental

1. Purpose of the contract

This contract sets out the terms and conditions for the rental of the Maxus 21 sailing boat (hereinafter “the Boat”) located at Harbour de Cheyres on Lake Neuchâtel, Switzerland, between the Lessor (Grégory Undritz) and the Lessee.

2. Description of the boat and equipment

The boat is hired with a full safety kit in accordance with Swiss legislation (LNI), an outboard motor, and a set of sails (mainsail and jib).

3. Security deposit

A security deposit of CHF 1,000 is required prior to boarding. As the boat does not have comprehensive insurance, this deposit is intended primarily to cover:

  • Material damage, damage, loss or deterioration caused to the boat and its equipment (sails, engine, hull, safety equipment).
  • An unjustified late return or abandonment of the boat in another harbour.

If the cost of repairs or replacement exceeds the amount of the deposit, the balance remains entirely the responsibility of the hirer (see Article 6). The deposit will be refunded following a satisfactory return inspection.

4. Cancellation terms and weather conditions

Cancellation by the hirer: In the event of cancellation less than 30 days before departure, 50% of the rental amount is due.

Weather conditions: In the event of dangerous weather (storm warning confirmed by the lessor), the rental may be postponed. No refund will be granted for light rain or lack of wind.

5. Obligations of the tenant (Skipper)

  • The tenant declares that they hold the required sailing licence for Swiss waters.
  • The renter is responsible for the boat and its crew throughout the rental period.
  • Subletting or lending the boat is strictly prohibited.
  • In the event of damage, the lessor must be contacted immediately.

6. Insurance and liability in the event of damage

Lessor’s Insurance: The Boat is covered solely by Third-Party Liability Insurance (TPL). This insurance covers bodily injury and property damage caused to third parties by the Boat.

No Hull Insurance (Damage to the Boat): The Boat does not have hull insurance (neither partial nor collision cover). Consequently, all material damage, damage, loss or deterioration occurring to the Boat itself or its equipment during the rental period is the sole responsibility of the Renter.

Coverage of damage by the Renter: In the event of an accident or damage to the Boat, the costs will first be deducted from the deposit of CHF 1,000. If the costs exceed this amount, the Renter undertakes to cover the full amount of the difference. It is the Tenant’s responsibility to ensure that their own Personal Liability Insurance (PLI) or specific skipper’s insurance expressly covers damage caused to hired boats.

Accidents and personal effects: Accident insurance for occupants and cover for the crew’s personal effects are not included in the rental and are the responsibility of each crew member’s personal insurance.

7. Inventory and condition report

An inventory and inspection (check-in/check-out) will be carried out on departure and return. The hirer undertakes to return the boat in the same clean and operational condition as on collection.
